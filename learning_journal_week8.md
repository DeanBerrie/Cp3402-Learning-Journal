# Learning Experiment:

### Hypothesis
- Using active memory/ recalling details of current work without notes a few days after learning then checking if im right will help cement my understanding of the topics I am learning

### Day 1-3
- Here I noticed that at the start I had trouble recalling exact details or specificties relating to the work I even did the previous day
- Each day I had highlighted key terms or processes relating to this weeks and tried to remember the rest of the process or information about that area of study usually getting an answer and double checking straight away to make sure im getting the right answer

### Day 3-6
- I spent the first section re-reading or understand the key topics that I need to recall the details of
- Then I got an empty doc with no notes and went through each piece of information I wanted to remember and after writing details of each process or term, I then chekced afterwards and noticed the improvement from the first few days

### Measurement
- I measured by two factors
- The first being the amount of correct answers I got each day, and how I needed to think about questions/research before hand to remember the questions.
- The second being confidence in answering questions, there was no unsure answers towards the end of the week


## Results
- There was a clear imporvement throughout the week, earlier in the week I remember far less key points and often got details wrong or muddled up.
- However towards the end of the being able to confidently answer the questions and explain concepts easier and more clearly.


## Learning Insights

- This experiment showed me that how I study matters just as much as how long I study. Passive methods like rereading can feel easier, but they don’t challenge my brain enough to strengthen memory. Active recall forces me to retrieve information, which improves understanding and long-term retention.

- I also learned that adding a delay before testing myself (like 24 hours) is important because it shows what I actually remember, not just what is fresh in my mind.

- In future weeks, I plan to:

   - Use active recall regularly instead of relying on rereading
   - Combine it with spaced repetition to improve memory even more
   - Create practice questions to test myself more effectively

- Overall, this experiment helped me become more aware of effective learning strategies and how to apply them in my studies
